100-Day Timeline

September 1, 1939
Germany Invades Poland
Wehrmacht crosses Polish border at 4:45 AM. World War II begins.
September 3, 1939
Britain & France Declare War
Allied powers honor Polish guarantees, declare war on Germany.
September 17, 1939
Soviet Union Invades Poland
Red Army attacks from east, implementing secret Molotov-Ribbentrop provisions.
September 28, 1939
Soviet-Estonian Pact Signed
Estonia forced to sign "Pact of Mutual Assistance" allowing Soviet military bases.
September 29, 1939
Warsaw Surrenders
Polish capital falls to German forces after heroic defense.
September 30, 1939
Hitler Issues Directive No. 5
Führer orders pivot from Eastern to Western front strategy.
October 1, 1939
COORDINATION DAY
• Churchill delivers "riddle wrapped in mystery" speech
• Soviet troops prepare Estonian base occupation
• US 67th Armor 2nd Battalion reactivated
• Saaremaa & Hiiumaa Islands strategic importance emerges
October 5, 1939
Soviet-Latvian Pact
Latvia forced to accept similar "mutual assistance" agreement.
October 10, 1939
Soviet-Lithuanian Pact
Lithuania becomes third Baltic state under Soviet influence.
October 12, 1939
Soviet Bases Operational
Red Army establishes operational presence in all three Baltic states.
November 30, 1939
Winter War Begins
Soviet Union invades Finland after failed negotiations.
December 10, 1939
Baltic Sea Soviet-Controlled
Combined German-Soviet control effectively closes Baltic to Allied operations.
