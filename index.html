Inner Planets

Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars - rocky worlds formed from the condensation of metals and silicates close to the Sun. Earth is the only known planet with life.

Asteroid Belt

Millions of rocky remnants between Mars and Jupiter. These are building blocks that never formed a planet due to Jupiter's gravitational influence.

Gas Giants

Jupiter and Saturn - massive worlds made mostly of hydrogen and helium. Jupiter has 95 known moons and acts as a "cosmic vacuum cleaner" protecting inner planets.

Ice Giants

Uranus and Neptune - distant worlds with water, methane, and ammonia ice. Neptune has winds reaching 2,100 km/h, the fastest in the solar system.

Kuiper Belt

A region of icy bodies beyond Neptune including dwarf planets like Pluto. These frozen remnants preserve materials from the solar system's formation.
